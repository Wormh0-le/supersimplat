# Move the distractor-enclosed rear cap to ambiguous Ground Truth via geometric sight-line screening

The controlled front/back-overlap acceptance gate requires rear-surface recall of at least 0.90, but the frozen fixture made that mathematically impossible. The rear Ground Truth was defined by the mechanical rule `z > 0` (4,095 target Gaussians), while the distractor shell — deliberately placed offset behind the target to create the front/back-overlap stress — encloses the target's rear polar cap: every external sight line to it crosses at least one 0.96-opacity distractor layer. Measured on the sealed multipoint-v3 trial, the cap's positive evidence lands at median 0.56, maximum 4.02, against the ~4 needed for the 0.8 selection posterior, so the locked Evidence Policy honestly cannot select it. The absolute rear-recall ceiling with every `z ≤ 0.3` rear Gaussian selected is 0.882. The specification already requires the opposite behavior elsewhere: its Honest-coverage criterion demands that insufficiently observable Gaussians remain Uncertain with the limitation disclosed, and its Benchmark Ground Truth definition provides an ambiguous class excluded from accuracy rather than forced into either class. The fixture, the completeness gate, and the honesty criterion could not hold simultaneously; issue #31 decided that the defect is the Ground Truth scope, not the method.

The rear-surface Ground Truth is therefore redefined to the honestly observable rear surface, and the distractor-enclosed cap moves to ambiguous Ground Truth. The boundary rule is geometric and method-independent: the fixture generator screens each target Gaussian against a fixed 1024-direction Fibonacci sphere and classifies it as ambiguous if and only if no direction gives it an unobstructed sight line past every distractor Gaussian. A sight line is obstructed when the ray meets a distractor Gaussian's occlusion sphere — every fixture Gaussian is isotropic (identity rotation, one log-scale), so each Gaussian's ellipsoid is a sphere and ray-ellipsoid testing is ray-sphere testing — whose radius is the covering radius of the actual distractor point set over a dense deterministic oversampling of its ellipsoid surface — 0.022093, about 1.004σ, the smallest radius whose per-Gaussian spheres leave no gap in the shell sheet. The rule reads fixture geometry only; it never consults trial output or the Evidence Policy, preserving the method-independence Benchmark Ground Truth requires. Cross-checked against the sealed-trial diagnosis, the screening finds 288 fully enclosed Gaussians, all inside the measured 482-cap (their z range [0.311, 0.344] refines the cap's approximate z > 0.3 boundary); the remaining 194 measured-cap Gaussians keep at least one genuinely unobstructed sight line through the annular gap between the two shells and remain selected truth that the method must actually observe. The rule is stable: the enclosed set is identical at 2,048 directions, differs by two rim Gaussians (4158, 4501) at 4,096, and grows slowly with the occlusion radius (+6, +16, +42 at ×1.05, ×1.10, ×1.25) as the rim continuum joins. The frozen definition is the declared rule itself — 1024 directions at the covering radius — not an approximation of a larger sample.

Ground Truth re-froze as revision 2: 288 ambiguous IDs, rear surface 3,807, selected truth 7,904, with the screening rule, covering-radius derivation, and disclosure recorded in the Ground Truth metadata. The scene PLY and the frozen five-point Benchmark Prompt Log are byte-identical (digests `cb238cb7…` and `8cce1991…` unchanged); the four 0.9 gates, the ≤81 Overlap Safety Gate, the distractor shell, Evidence Policy v1, Coverage Report semantics, and the scorer are unchanged — the scorer already excluded ambiguous truth and already requires the rear surface to be a subset of selected truth, an invariant that still holds. Disclosure lands at the benchmark-record layer (Ground Truth metadata, fixture documentation, this ADR, and trial run records); runtime coverage semantics were deliberately not revised, because the exclusion already removes the cap from scoring and a policy revision would recalibrate the locked path for no verdict benefit. The sealed multipoint-v3 record (IoU 0.805, precision 0.995, recall 0.808, rear 0.642, distractors 32) remains valid historical evidence under the superseded Ground Truth revision 1; old predictions are not re-scored against revision 2 to claim a pass, and fresh blind trials seal and score against revision 2.

The rejected alternatives: recalibrating the fixture geometry (option 1) destroys the designed front/back-overlap difficulty and forces re-proof that a new geometry is neither trivial nor impossible, invalidating every calibration tied to this fixture; lowering the gate (option 3) fits the threshold to the geometry accident after seeing scores — benchmark tuning the project forbids — and is simultaneously too lax for the observable rear, where a working method scores near 1.0; accepting the failure as the verdict (option 4) reports a measured fixture-scope defect as a method failure and corrupts the PoC's honest pass/fail purpose.

Two consequences traveled beyond the Ground Truth files. The shared-lifting diagnostic graph hash-bound the old Ground Truth npz, so per its own immutability convention the correction produced a new `shared-lifting-benchmark-v2.json`; the v1 manifest keeps its committed bytes as the historical record that existing diagnostic results bind to, and `targets.json` now binds v2. Separately, the office and controlled-overlap fixture graphs carried a pre-existing digest drift of the Prettier-rewrite class — digest fields bound to pre-Prettier bytes of JSON files, visible since the fixtures' import commit. The repair rebound those fields to the current Prettier-canonical bytes, verified level by level: every rebound reference is JSON and Prettier-canonical, and every binary reference (masks, frames, coverage tensors, Ground Truth npz files) matched its recorded digest byte-exactly, confirming the drift was formatting-only. The fixture freeze procedure in `docs/benchmarks/fixtures/controlled-overlap/README.md` now records the digest-after-Prettier ordering that prevents this class.
